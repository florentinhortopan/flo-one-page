Leading design system development and accessibility compliance across Gap's multi-brand portfolio

Role: Design System Lead and Manager
Duration: Apr 2019 - Aug 2021
Location: San Francisco, CA

The Challenge: Gap Inc. needed a unified design system to serve multiple brands (Gap, Banana Republic, Old Navy, Athleta) across web, mobile, and native apps while maintaining brand identity and ensuring accessibility compliance.

My Role: I led a small design team to develop and release Stitch, Gap's new design system, managing the entire pipeline from component taxonomy to Figma migration and accessibility standardization.

Multi-Brand Design Challenge

Gap Inc. operates four major retail brands, each with distinct visual identities but shared e-commerce infrastructure. The challenge was creating a scalable design system that could serve all brands while reducing redundancy and ensuring consistent, accessible experiences.

The Problem

Before Stitch, Gap Inc.'s design ecosystem was fragmented and inefficient:

Key Challenges

  • Component libraries scattered across multiple design tools (Sketch, Adobe XD, Figma)
  • Inconsistent component patterns across brands and platforms
  • No centralized style guide or component documentation
  • Accessibility compliance gaps across brands (U.S. and Canada standards)
  • Slow design-to-development handoff due to specification inconsistencies
  • Designers and developers using outdated or deprecated components

This fragmentation led to duplicated effort, inconsistent user experiences, potential legal liability from accessibility non-compliance, and slowed product development velocity.

The Solution: Stitch Design System

I led the development of Stitch, a comprehensive design system that unified components, established clear governance, and ensured accessibility compliance across all Gap Inc. brands.

Accessibility Compliance

A critical part of my role was ensuring all components met strict legal requirements for accessibility standards in both the United States and Canada, significantly reducing the company's legal liability.

Accessibility Standards Met

  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance for all components
  • ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) compliance for U.S. operations
  • AODA (Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act) compliance for Canadian operations
  • Screen reader compatibility across all interactive components
  • Keyboard navigation support for all user flows
  • Color contrast ratios meeting or exceeding WCAG standards
