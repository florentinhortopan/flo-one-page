Case Study 01

Accessories E-commerce Experience

Redesigning the accessories discovery and purchase flow to help users find the right mounts, cases, and add-ons for their GoPro cameras.

The Problem

GoPro offers hundreds of accessories and mounts, but users struggled to find the right products for their specific use cases and camera models. The existing product pages lacked clear categorization, compatibility information, and contextual guidance.

Key Challenges

  • Users couldn't easily filter accessories by activity or camera compatibility
  • Product pages lacked visual context showing accessories in use
  • Search functionality was keyword-dependent with no smart suggestions
  • High bounce rates on landing pages due to overwhelming choice

Research & Discovery

I conducted user research to understand customer behaviors and needs when shopping for GoPro accessories. This included analyzing user flows, click-through data, and customer support inquiries.

Case Study 02

Support Page Redesign

Streamlining the customer support experience to help users find answers faster and reduce support ticket volume.

The Problem

Users struggled to navigate GoPro's support pages, leading to frustration and high support ticket volumes. The information architecture was complex, and users couldn't quickly find solutions to common issues.

Research Insights

  • 70% of support tickets were for issues already documented in FAQs
  • Users bounced from support pages within 30 seconds on average
  • Search functionality returned irrelevant results
  • No clear path from problem identification to solution

Research & User Journey Mapping

I conducted extensive UX research to map user journeys through the support system and identify pain points.

User journey mapping: From homepage to support resolution

ðŸ“„ UX Research - Support Page Analysis

The Solution

I redesigned the support experience with a focus on intelligent content organization, improved search, and guided troubleshooting flows.

Interactive prototype: New support page design with intelligent search and categorization

Key Features

  • Smart search with auto-suggestions based on camera model and issue type
  • Visual troubleshooting guides with step-by-step instructions
  • Product-specific support pathways
  • Community-driven Q&A integration
  • Escalation path to live chat when self-service fails

Case Study 03

GoPro PLUS Strategy

Exploring different strategic approaches to position and promote GoPro's premium subscription service.

The Challenge

GoPro PLUS offered cloud storage, unlimited camera replacement, and exclusive discounts, but struggled with awareness and conversion. We needed to explore different positioning strategies and user experience approaches to drive subscription growth.

Strategic Approaches

I created prototypes exploring different value propositions and UX patterns to understand what would resonate most with GoPro users.

Prototype exploration: Multiple strategic approaches to GoPro PLUS positioning

Strategies Explored

  • Camera replacement insurance as primary value proposition
  • Cloud-first approach highlighting unlimited backup
  • Community & exclusive content membership model
  • Discounts & perks as main driver
  • All-in-one comprehensive value bundle

User Insights & Context

Recommendations

Based on user testing and strategic analysis, I recommended a hybrid approach that leads with camera replacement insurance for acquisition, then highlights cloud storage for retention, with discounts as a supporting benefit.
