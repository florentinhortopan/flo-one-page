INOA

Flooded Scuba Mask with Interchangeable Optics

The Challenge: Designing a flooded scuba mask with interchangeable optics to solve underwater vision problems and enhance diving safety.

My Role: Led the optical design and engineering of interchangeable lens systems, creating a revolutionary approach to underwater vision correction and enhancement.

Underwater Vision Innovation

The INOA project addresses a critical problem in underwater diving: vision distortion and correction in flooded mask conditions. Traditional scuba masks fail when flooded, creating dangerous situations for divers.

Technical Innovation

  • Interchangeable Optics: Modular lens system allowing divers to swap optics based on diving conditions
  • Flooded Mask Design: Revolutionary approach that maintains vision clarity even when mask fills with water
  • Optical Correction: Advanced lens technology compensating for underwater refraction and distortion
  • Safety Enhancement: Reducing panic and improving diver confidence in emergency situations

Research & Development

This project involved extensive research into underwater optics, refraction principles, and human factors in diving safety. The interchangeable optics system represents a breakthrough in underwater vision technology, with applications extending beyond recreational diving to professional underwater operations.

Impact & Recognition

The INOA flooded mask design has been featured in scientific publications including Applied Optics and SPIE Proceedings, recognizing its contribution to underwater vision research and diver safety innovation.
