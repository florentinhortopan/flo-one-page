Intellectual Property in Wearable Tech

Innovation Protection: Patents representing novel approaches to integrating electronics into wearable products, from smart textiles to practical accessories. These innovations bridge fashion, function, and emerging technology.

2014

Method and Process for Embedding Practical and Decorative Electronics to Garments and Wearable Accessories

U.S. Patent
Patent ID: EFS ID 21841233; 62137231
Status: Filed
Category: Smart Textiles & Wearable Electronics

Innovation: Novel method for integrating flexible electronic components into fabric-based products while maintaining garment functionality, washability, and safety standards.

Key Technical Contributions:

  • → Process for safely encapsulating electronic components within textiles
  • → Methods for creating washable electrical connections in flexible materials
  • → Design approaches for balancing aesthetics with electronic functionality
  • → Manufacturing processes suitable for both decorative and functional applications
Real-World Application: Developed from practical experience creating tshirtOS™ (Techtextil Innovation Award 2015) and Agent of Presence luxury wearables. Patent protects novel manufacturing and design methods that enable electronics to be integrated into garments without compromising comfort, safety, or washability.

Industry Impact: Addresses critical challenges in wearable technology by providing practical solutions for manufacturers seeking to integrate electronics into textile products at scale.

2008

i5hot Convertible Gloves

Design Patent
Patent ID: UIBM FI2008/0/000029
Jurisdiction: Italy (UIBM - Ufficio Italiano Brevetti e Marchi)
Status: Registered
Category: Accessory Design

Innovation: Convertible glove design enabling touchscreen device operation without conductive fabrics—predating widespread adoption of touchscreen-compatible gloves.

Design Features:

  • → Convertible thumb and index finger for direct screen contact
  • → 11-gauge pure cashmere construction maintaining luxury feel
  • → Functional mechanism integrated into traditional glove construction
  • → Available in multiple sizes (S/M/L) with Made in Italy manufacturing
Market Timing: Patented in 2008, addressing the emerging need for touchscreen-compatible winter accessories. Simple mechanical solution predated the later industry standard of conductive fabric fingertips.

Product Impact: Successfully manufactured and sold 2007–2010, demonstrating commercial viability of the patented design. Represents early recognition of touchscreen device usage patterns and accessory needs.

Innovation Philosophy

Patents should protect practical innovations that solve real problems. Both patents emerged from hands-on product development, addressing genuine technical and market challenges rather than theoretical possibilities. The goal is always to create intellectual property that enables better products, not to patent for the sake of patenting.
